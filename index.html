Forming the subjunctive (presente do subjuntivo)

The present subjunctive in Brazilian Portuguese is built from the first-person singular (eu) of the present indicative. Drop the -o and add the subjunctive endings — this rule covers the vast majority of verbs, including many that are irregular in the indicative.

The golden rule: take the eu form of the presente do indicativo → remove the final -o → add the subjunctive endings.

In spoken Brazilian Portuguese, a gente (we) is used far more than nós, and always takes the 3rd-person singular subjunctive form.

When to use the subjunctive
1. Wishes, desires, hopes

After verbs of wanting, wishing, hoping — when the subject changes between clauses.

Quero que você venha cedo. — I want you to come early.
2. Emotions and feelings

After expressions of joy, sadness, fear, surprise.

Fico feliz que você esteja bem. — I'm glad you're doing well.
3. Doubt and uncertainty
Duvido que ele saiba a resposta. — I doubt he knows the answer.
4. Impersonal expressions
É importante que a gente pratique todo dia. — It's important that we practice every day.

6. Future time clauses with quando, se, assim que
Quando você chegar, me liga. — When you arrive, call me.
Se ele vier amanhã, a gente conversa. — If he comes tomorrow, we'll talk.
7. Hypothetical conditions (imperfect subjunctive)
Se eu tivesse dinheiro, compraria uma casa. — If I had money, I'd buy a house.
8. Indirect 3rd-person commands
Que ele entre! — Let him come in!
After talvez (perhaps/maybe), the subjunctive is standard, though in very informal speech some Brazilians use the indicative.
