mesmo que / ainda que

Portuguese concessive conjunctions — integrated study guide with audio

Both expressions introduce a concessive idea — something that contrasts with the main statement but does not prevent it from happening.

ainda que tends to express a more factual or real concession — closer to "although" or "even though".

mesmo que tends to express a hypothetical or stronger emphatic concession — closer to "even if".

They are frequently interchangeable in everyday use, though mesmo que often feels more conditional or emphatic. Both usually appear with the subjunctive mood in the subordinate clause.

Comparison with Spanish and Italian

Spanish uses a single versatile word (aunque) that covers both factual and hypothetical cases.

Italian distinguishes more clearly: sebbene / benché for factual concession and anche se as the common expression for hypothetical "even if".
